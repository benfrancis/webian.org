About

Mission Statement

There's a growing trend of web applications replacing desktop applications, a migration from the "desktop" to the "cloud". New devices like smartphones, tablets, netbooks and set-top boxes are starting to support a new standard called HTML5 which is going to transform the web we know into something far richer and more interactive.

Do you have a collection of documents, images, audio and video measuring in the gigabytes or terabytes? It's pretty cheap for you to store this information yourself and you may like the fact that doing so means you have full control over your own data. But there's currently no affordable or easy way of accessing this data online in a way which takes full advantage of this rich new web experience.

The mission of the Webian project is to build an open source social media server for your home with a rich HTML5 web interface. Webian should make it easy to access all of your media online from a range of devices like phones, tablets, netbooks and your TV.

Read more in the introductory blog post.

Webian Principles

  1. Webian software should be related to the World Wide Web.
  2. Webian software shall always remain free - both free as in price and free as in freedom.
  3. Webian software should always promote web standards and open standards over proprietary technology.

The Webian project is a not-for-profit venture founded by Ben Francis. Ben is a full time software engineer at a Web 2.0 startup in Cambridge (England) and has an MEng degree in Computer Interactive Systems.
